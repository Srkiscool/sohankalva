Developing a Data Visualization Tool

Capital One is a financial corporation who specializes in normal banking stuff. I’m pretty sure you know what they do lol.

When stimulus checks were released during the pandemic many people tried to cash it in as soon as possible. This created a surge of Activity for many systems within Capital One’s network. The numerous teams who handled the upkeep of the network attempted to preemptively mitigate the foreseen problem through spinning up additional servers to balance out the load. However this still did not seem to work as the countless transactions still overwhelmed the system and slowed down the network to a snail pace. After incident reports were produced, management needed to comprehend this data to create the proper steps required to stop network latency from occurring. The only problem was that the incident reports were very hard to understand and far from literate since some sections needed to be manually filled out. The task was to create a data visualization which management could easily parse through and would aid in making comprehensive mitigatory steps.

After cleaning and formatting the data through custom scripts and the pandas library, the data was uploaded to an S3 bucket for later use. This was then connected to a redis cache layer for efficiency purposes. After which was later connected to a React application using the D3 and NVD3 visualization libraries. The end product ended up being even more useful than expected since the way the application was developed made it a universal visualization tool that anyone can connect their data to and use.
